GeoAI and machine learning

AI models now classify land cover from satellite imagery automatically, identify species from camera trap photos, and predict habitat corridor locations before development closes them off. The GeoAI market is growing at 31% annually.

🛸

Drones and LiDAR

Drone imagery and LiDAR now enable centimeter resolution habitat mapping at a fraction of the cost of traditional aerial surveys. Conservation teams are using these tools to monitor restoration projects and detect invasive species in real time.

☁️

Cloud GIS platforms

ArcGIS Online and Google Earth Engine removed the need for expensive hardware. A student with a free account can now run analysis that used to require weeks of desktop processing. The barrier to doing real conservation GIS has never been lower.

🌿

Citizen science at scale

iNaturalist now has over 200 million observations. GBIF has over 2 billion georeferenced records. These platforms are feeding live data into conservation models in real time and making spatial biodiversity analysis accessible to anyone.
