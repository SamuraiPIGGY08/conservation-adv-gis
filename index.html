  • Introduced layering spatial data to find patterns

1972
NASA Landsat launches
  • First satellite built for continuous Earth observation
  • Enabled monitoring of deforestation and land change from space
  • Built the longest environmental record that exists anywhere
  • Changed what conservation could see and track over time

1982
Esri releases ArcInfo
  • First commercially successful GIS software
  • Made spatial analysis accessible outside of government
  • Conservation organizations adopted it for habitat mapping
  • Jack Dangermond founded Esri in 1969 with conservation in mind

USGS NLCD and gap analysis
  • USGS publishes the National Land Cover Database
  • Standardized land cover maps available to any researcher
  • Gap analysis identifies ecosystems with no protection
  • Michael Goodchild established GIS as a scientific discipline
